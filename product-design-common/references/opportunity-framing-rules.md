# Opportunity Framing Rules

Start with outcome and customer problem before solution.

Good opportunity:
- customer-owned pain/need/desire
- tied to a product or business outcome
- observable or testable
- not a feature disguised as a problem

Good solution hypothesis:
- addresses one opportunity
- can be prototyped or tested cheaply
- names risk and success signal

Avoid feature-factory framing: do not turn every stakeholder ask directly into screens.
