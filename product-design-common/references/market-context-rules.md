# Market Context Rules

Read enough context to prevent fantasy design; do not become a news desk, TAM essay, or GTM team.

## Default Evidence Pack

Required for a promotion-style concept package:
- Tier A feasibility/constraint source: official docs, API docs, standards, regulations, institutional policy, or equivalent authority
- Tier B opponent/vendor sources: strongest direct opponent plus at least one regional alternative, indirect alternative, or current workaround
- Tier C real-user pain signals: at least two preserved reviews/forums/support/public complaints, labelled as signals and not proof
- smallest relevant standards: usually WCAG 2.2, NN/g, process frame, privacy/data feasibility, and one region/platform standard when relevant

Optional by default: TAM/SAM/SOM, trend essays, exhaustive competitor matrices, news scans, surveys/interviews, campaign/channel research.

## Market wedge gate

Before solution work, require:
- `beachhead_segment`, launch region/beachhead, `use_moment`, `excluded_users`, `local_constraints`
- `opponent_map` with at least one direct opponent and one workaround/indirect alternative
- `strongest_direct_opponent`, `why_users_choose_opponent_today`, `opponent_coverage_gap`
- `pain_evidence_status`, `customer_pull_signal`, `market_gate_verdict`, `kill_or_pivot_condition`
- `novelty_delta_hypothesis`, `delta_against_opponent`, and `not_the_novelty`

Novelty is not uniqueness. It is a hypothesis that this beachhead has a materially better way through one painful moment than the strongest current alternative.

Separate:
- fact: directly supported by reliable source
- source claim: vendor/project claim not independently verified
- signal: weak market/social/community clue
- inference: synthesis from evidence
- assumption: needed but unverified
- gap: evidence missing before confidence

Stop once context can support the brief. If core feasibility, opponent, or user-need evidence is missing, mark degraded/blocked and route to validation.

## Canvas student planner example
If the concept involves Canvas, timetable, coursework, or student planning, include official Canvas Planner/API feasibility, student-facing workflow references, direct competitors, similar GitHub repos, and campus/local constraints before claiming promotion readiness.
