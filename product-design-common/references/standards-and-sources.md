# Standards and Sources

Use the smallest relevant subset. Capture links in `01-sources.md` and map decisions in `02-standards.md`.

## Source quality tiers

- Tier A — authoritative: official docs, APIs, regulations, standards, institutional policies. Supports facts, constraints, feasibility.
- Tier B — opponent/vendor: product pages, docs, pricing, case studies. Supports “they claim / offer / pattern exists,” not effectiveness.
- Tier C — user/community signal: reviews, forums, GitHub issues, support threads, social posts. Supports pain signals only; mark “not proof.”
- Tier D — secondary context: blogs, listicles, journalism, analyst posts. Context only; do not support core promotion claims.
- Tier E — unusable: dead, blocked, AI summary, uncited, inaccessible. Gap/blocker only.

`01-sources.md` table contract: `url | source_role | quality_tier | claim | opponent/pain signal | decision impact | supports_promotion | retrieval_date | verification_status`.

Promotion needs enough A/B/C evidence and no Tier D/E source supporting a promotion claim. Dead/blocked links may remain as gaps only.
